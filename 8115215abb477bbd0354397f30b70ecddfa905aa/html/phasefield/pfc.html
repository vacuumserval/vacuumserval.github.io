    Phase Field Crystal (2D)¶

    Mode Expansion¶

    Triangular (hexagonal) lattice¶

    the lattice vectors¶

    • the lattice vectors:

    \[\begin{split}\begin{align} \begin{cases} a_1 &= \left(-\frac{2a}{\sqrt{3}}, 0\right) \nonumber \\ a_2 &= \left(-\frac{a}{\sqrt{3}}, a\right) \nonumber \end{cases} \end{align}\end{split}\]

    • the reciprocal lattice vectors (RLVs):

    \[\begin{split}\begin{align} \begin{cases} b_1 &= \left(-\frac{\sqrt{3}\pi}{a}, -\frac{\pi}{a}\right) \nonumber \\ b_2 &= \left(0, \frac{2\pi}{a}\right) \nonumber \end{cases} \end{align}\end{split}\]

    the magnitude of the principal RLVs is \(\frac{2\pi}{a}\)

    the mode expansion¶

    The density \(nlst\) of the crystalline state can be expressed in terms of the reciprocal lattice vectors (RLVs) \(\mathbf{k}\)’s

    \[\begin{align} nlst(r) = n_0+\sum_\mathbf{k}A_ke^{i\mathbf{k}\cdot\mathbf{r}} \end{align}\]

    • 1st mode

    The 1st mode consists of \((1,0), (0,1), (-1,-1)\):

    \[\begin{align} \sum_\mathbf{k_1} A_{1}e^{i\mathbf{k_1} \cdot\mathbf{r}} =A_1\left[ 2 \cos \left(\frac{\pi \sqrt{3} x}{a}+\frac{\pi y}{a}\right)+2 \cos \left(\frac{\sqrt{3} \pi x}{a}-\frac{\pi y}{a}\right)+2 \cos \left(\frac{2 \pi y}{a}\right)\right] \end{align}\]

    • 2nd mode

    The 2nd mode consists of \((-1,-2), (-2,-1), (-1,1)\):

    \[\begin{align} \sum_\mathbf{k_2} A_{2}e^{i\mathbf{k_2} \cdot\mathbf{r}} = A_2\left[2 \cos \left(\frac{\pi \sqrt{3} x}{a}+\frac{3 \pi y}{a}\right)+2 \cos \left(\frac{\sqrt{3} \pi x}{a}-\frac{3 \pi y}{a}\right)+2 \cos \left(\frac{2 \sqrt{3} \pi x}{a}\right)\right] \end{align}\]

    So the density \(nlst\) is

    \[\begin{split}\begin{align} n_t(r) &= n_0+\sum_\mathbf{k}A_ke^{i\mathbf{k}\cdot\mathbf{r}} \approx n_0+A_{1}\sum_\mathbf{k_1} e^{i\mathbf{k_1} \cdot\mathbf{r}}+ A_{2}\sum_\mathbf{k_2}e^{i\mathbf{k_2} \cdot\mathbf{r}}\nonumber \\ &=n_0+A_1\left[ 2 \cos \left(\frac{\pi \sqrt{3} x}{a}+\frac{\pi y}{a}\right)+2 \cos \left(\frac{\sqrt{3} \pi x}{a}-\frac{\pi y}{a}\right)+2 \cos \left(\frac{2 \pi y}{a}\right)\right] \nonumber \\ &+A_2\left[2 \cos \left(\frac{\pi \sqrt{3} x}{a}+\frac{3 \pi y}{a}\right)+2 \cos \left(\frac{\sqrt{3} \pi x}{a}-\frac{3 \pi y}{a}\right)+2 \cos \left(\frac{2 \sqrt{3} \pi x}{a}\right)\right] \end{align}\end{split}\]

    in PFC implementations, \(A_2\) is set to be \(0\).

    Square lattice¶

    the lattice vectors¶

    • the lattice vectors:

    \[\begin{split}\begin{align} \begin{cases} a_1 &= \left(a, 0\right) \nonumber \\ a_2 &= \left(0, a\right) \nonumber \end{cases} \end{align}\end{split}\]

    • the reciprocal lattice vectors (RLVs):

    \[\begin{split}\begin{align} \begin{cases} b_1 &= \left(\frac{2\pi}{a}, 0\right) \nonumber \\ b_2 &= \left(0, \frac{2\pi}{a}\right) \nonumber \end{cases} \end{align}\end{split}\]

    the magnitude of the principal RLVs is \(\frac{2\pi}{a}\)

    the mode expansion¶

    The density \(n\) of the crystalline state can be expressed in terms of the reciprocal lattice vectors (RLVs) \(\mathbf{k}\)’s

    \[\begin{align} n(r) = n_0+\sum_\mathbf{k}A_ke^{i\mathbf{k}\cdot\mathbf{r}} \end{align}\]

    • 1st mode

    The 1st mode consists of \((1,0), (0,1)\):

    \[\begin{align} \sum_\mathbf{k_1} A_{1}e^{i\mathbf{k_1} \cdot\mathbf{r}} =A_1\left[2 \cos \left(\frac{2 \pi x}{a}\right)+2 \cos \left(\frac{2 \pi y}{a}\right)\right] \end{align}\]

    • 2nd mode

    The 2nd mode consists of \((1,1), (1,-1)\):

    \[\begin{align} \sum_\mathbf{k_2} A_{2}e^{i\mathbf{k_2} \cdot\mathbf{r}} = A_2\left[2 \cos \left(\frac{2 \pi x}{a}+\frac{2 \pi y}{a}\right)+2 \cos \left(\frac{2 \pi x}{a}-\frac{2 \pi y}{a}\right)\right] \end{align}\]

    So the density \(nlst\) is

    \[\begin{split}\begin{align} n_s(r) &= n_0+\sum_\mathbf{k}A_ke^{i\mathbf{k}\cdot\mathbf{r}} \approx n_0+A_{1}\sum_\mathbf{k_1} e^{i\mathbf{k_1} \cdot\mathbf{r}}+ A_{2}\sum_\mathbf{k_2}e^{i\mathbf{k_2} \cdot\mathbf{r}}\nonumber \\ &=n_0+A_1\left[2 \cos \left(\frac{2 \pi x}{a}\right)+2 \cos \left(\frac{2 \pi y}{a}\right)\right]+A_2\left[2 \cos \left(\frac{2 \pi x}{a}+\frac{2 \pi y}{a}\right)+2 \cos \left(\frac{2 \pi x}{a}-\frac{2 \pi y}{a}\right)\right] \end{align}\end{split}\]

    in PFC implementations, \(A_2\neq 0\), the exact value need to be determined through further calculation.

    Free Energy Functional¶

    XPFC¶

    The XPFC free energy functional consists the ideal energy contribution \(F_{id}\) and the excess energy contribution \(F_{ex}\):

    \[\begin{split}\begin{align} F_{id}&=\int_V d\mathbf{r}\left[\frac{n^2(\mathbf{r})}{2}-\eta\frac{n^3(\mathbf{r})}{6}+\chi\frac{n^4(\mathbf{r})}{12}\right] \\ F_{ex}&=-\frac{1}{2}\int_V d\mathbf{r}\left[n(\mathbf{r})\int_V d\mathbf{r}'\left[C_2(\mathbf{r}-\mathbf{r}')n(\mathbf{r}')\right]\right] \end{align}\end{split}\]

    By adopting the convolution theorem, \(C_2(\mathbf{r}-\mathbf{r}')n(\mathbf{r}')=\mathcal{F}^{-1}\left(\hat{C}_2(\mathbf{k})\cdot\hat{n}(\mathbf{k})\right)\), where \(\hat{C}_2(\mathbf{k})\neq 0\) only for the length scale of the reciprocal family of planes.

    • Substitute the amorphous phase’s density \(n_a(\mathbf{r})\), the triangle phase’s density \(n_t(\mathbf{r})\), and the square phase’s density \(n_s(\mathbf{r})\) to the ideal energy \(F_{id}\), and divide by the unit volume/area to get the free energy density \(f(n)\):

    \[\begin{split}\begin{align} f_{id,a}(n) &= \frac{n^2}{2}-\eta \frac{n^3}{6}+ \chi \frac{n^4}{12} \\ f_{id,t}(n) &= \frac{n^2}{2}-\eta \frac{n^3}{6}+ \chi \frac{n^4}{12} + 3A_1^2(1-\eta n+\chi n^2) \nonumber \\ &-2A_1^3(\eta-2\chi n) + \frac{15A_1^4}{2}\chi \\ f_{id,s}(n) &= \frac{n^2}{2}-\eta \frac{n^3}{6}+ \chi \frac{n^4}{12} + 2(A_1^2+A_2^2)(1-\eta n+\chi n^2) \nonumber \\ &-4A_1^2A_2(\eta-2\chi n)+3(A_1^4+4A_1^2A_2^2+A_2^4)\chi \end{align}\end{split}\]

    it is not surprising to see that free energy density is NOT a function of lattice constant \(a\), because the free energy functional has nothing to do with the lattice constant.

    • For the excessed free energy part, the \(\mathbf{k}=k_0=0\) planes (0th mode) provide a \(n^2\hat{C}(k_0)\) term, the \(\mathbf{k}=k_1\) planes provide a \(A_1^2\hat{C}(k_1)\) term, and the \(\mathbf{k}=k_2\) planes provide a \(A_2^2\hat{C}(k_2)\) term. Such that

    \[\begin{split}\begin{align} f_{ex,a} &= -\frac{n^2}{2}\hat{C}(k_0) \\ f_{ex,t} &= -\frac{n^2}{2}\hat{C}(k_0) - 3A_1^2\hat{C}(k_1) \\ f_{ex,s} &= -\frac{n^2}{2}\hat{C}(k_0) - 2A_1^2\hat{C}(k_1) - 2A_2^2\hat{C}(k_2) \end{align}\end{split}\]

    minimize the functional¶

    The free energy density functionals should be minimized by tuning the amplitutdes.

    \[\begin{split}\begin{align} f_{a}(n) &= \frac{n^2}{2}-\eta \frac{n^3}{6}+ \chi \frac{n^4}{12} -\frac{n^2}{2}\hat{C}(k_0)\\ f_{t}(n) &= \frac{n^2}{2}-\eta \frac{n^3}{6}+ \chi \frac{n^4}{12} + 3A_1^2(1-\eta n+\chi n^2) \nonumber \\ &-2A_1^3(\eta-2\chi n) + \frac{15A_1^4}{2}\chi -\frac{n^2}{2}\hat{C}(k_0) - 3A_1^2\hat{C}(k_1)\\ f_{s}(n) &= \frac{n^2}{2}-\eta \frac{n^3}{6}+ \chi \frac{n^4}{12} + 2(A_1^2+A_2^2)(1-\eta n+\chi n^2) \nonumber \\ &-4A_1^2A_2(\eta-2\chi n)+3(A_1^4+4A_1^2A_2^2+A_2^4)\chi \nonumber \\ &-\frac{n^2}{2}\hat{C}(k_0) - 2A_1^2\hat{C}(k_1) - 2A_2^2\hat{C}(k_2) \end{align}\end{split}\]

    A numerical minimization for \(A\) yields:

    [65]:

    import numpy as np
import matplotlib.pyplot as plt

def faamp(nlst, eta, chi, C0):
    n = nlst
    f = n**2/2-eta*n**3/6+chi*n**4/12-n**2/2*C0
    return(n, f)

def ftamp(nlst, eta, chi, C0, C1):
    amax = 1.0
    aunit = 0.0025
    alim = 0.1
    alst = np.arange(0, amax+aunit, aunit)
    [a1, n] = np.meshgrid(alst, nlst, indexing='ij')
    fmat = n**2/2-eta*n**3/6+chi*n**4/12 \
        + 3*a1**2*(1-eta*n+chi*n**2) \
        - 2*a1**3*(eta-2*chi*n) + 7.5*a1**4*chi \
        - 0.5*n**2*C0-3*a1**2*C1
    fmin = np.min(fmat, axis=0)
    amin = alst[np.argmin(fmat, axis=0)]
    nt = nlst[amin>alim]
    ft = fmin[amin>alim]
    return(nt, ft)

def fsamp(nlst, eta, chi, C0, C1, C2):
    amax = 1.0
    aunit = 0.0025
    alim = int(0.1/aunit)
    alst = np.arange(0, amax+aunit, aunit)
    [a1, a2, n] = np.meshgrid(alst, alst, nlst, indexing='ij')
    fmat = n**2/2-eta*n**3/6+chi*n**4/12 \
        + 2*(a1**2+a2**2)*(1-eta*n+chi*n**2) \
        - 4*a1**2*a2*(eta-2*eta*n) + 3*(a1**4+4*a1**2*a2**2+a2**4)*chi \
        - 0.5*n**2*C0 - 2*a1**2*C1 - 2*a2**2*C2
    fmin = np.min(fmat, axis=(0, 1))
    as_index = []
    for i in range(fmat.shape[2]):
        min_index = np.unravel_index(np.argmin(fmat[:, :, i]), fmat[:, :, i].shape)
        a1nonzero = min_index[0]>alim
        a2nonzero = min_index[1]>alim
        as_index.append(a1nonzero*a2nonzero)
    ns = nlst[as_index]
    fs = fmin[as_index]
    return(ns, fs)

nlst = np.arange(-0.3, 0.5, 0.01)
[na, fa] = faamp(nlst=nlst, eta=1, chi=1, C0=0.0)
[nt, ft] = ftamp(nlst=nlst, eta=1, chi=1, C0=0.0, C1=0.919)
[ns, fs] = fsamp(nlst=nlst, eta=1, chi=1, C0=0.0, C1=0.919, C2=0.887)
plt.figure()
plt.plot(na, fa, label='amourphous')
plt.plot(nt, ft, label='triangle')
plt.plot(ns, fs, label='square')
plt.grid()
plt.legend()

    temperature and specific length¶

    The correlation parameter is where the temperature \(\sigma\) and specific length \(a\) goes in, according to GREENWOOD M, PROVATAS N, ROTTLER J. Free Energy Functionals for Efficient Phase Field Crystal Modeling of Structural Phase Transformations[J/OL]. Physical Review Letters, 2010, 105(4): 045702. DOI:10.1103/PhysRevLett.105.045702:

    \[\begin{align} \hat{C}(k)=\max_{\text{for each peak}} \left\{e^{-\frac{\sigma^2k_i^2}{2\rho_i\beta_i}}e^{-\frac{(k-k_i)^2}{2\alpha_i^2}}\right\} \end{align}\]

    However I believe there is a typo in \(e^{-\frac{\sigma^2k_i^2}{2\rho_i\beta_i}}\), where the \(k_i^2\) should be eliminated, by a numerical validation with fig. 1, it should be:

    \[\begin{align} \hat{C}(k)=\max_{\text{for each peak}} \left\{e^{-\frac{\sigma^2}{2\rho_i\beta_i}}e^{-\frac{(k-k_i)^2}{2\alpha_i^2}}\right\} \end{align}\]

    The specific length \(a\) can be set based on the simulated system, in Greenwood’s model, \(a=1\), \(k_1=\frac{2\pi}{a}\approx 6.28\), \(k_2=\frac{2\sqrt{2}\pi}{a}\approx 8.88\), \(\beta_1=\beta_2=4\), \(\rho_1=1\), \(\rho_2=1/\sqrt{2}\).

    For example, at high (Ch) and low (Cl) temperature, the peaks’ heights:
